Multiplane Diffusion Features (MDF): Depth-Aware Editing in Diffusion Models with Multiplane Representation

In this work, we propose a generalized zero-shot framework to perform depth-aware compositions, such as inserting objects at a certain depth with diffusion models. We integrate Multiplane Image representation - an effective depth-aware scene representation in the features space of diffusion models for realistic depth-aware editing. We propose Multiplane Diffusion Features (MDF), a multiplane representation of the denoising UNet's internal features. For editing, we introduce an MDF guidance mechanism that gradually guides the latents toward the intended target edit, preserving distributional consistency throughout the denoising process. We validate our approach on two depth-aware editing tasks: a) object insertion that preserves object and scene identity with seamless blending, and b) depth-based composition of two real scenes with accurate lighting.
